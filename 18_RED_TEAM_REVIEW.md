# Red-Team Review — Lesson Loom Context Pack v2

## Strongest parts of the concept

1. **Clear before/after:** static lesson plan becomes interactive classroom interface.
2. **High visual potential:** Fraction Garden can produce screenshots that feel warm, concrete, and distinctive.
3. **Strong Stitch fit:** natural-language input becomes interface output.
4. **Responsible stance:** teacher review, no student data, no automated grading.
5. **PrairieSignal reuse:** can become Labs proof, Research/Edge workshop demo, and education-facing artifact.

## Main risks

### Risk 1 — Looks like a lesson-plan generator

Mitigation:

- Always say “lesson interface builder,” not “curriculum generator.”
- Show teacher-provided plan as the source of truth.
- Make Review & Safety visible.

### Risk 2 — Visual design becomes childish

Mitigation:

- Use premium paper/editorial classroom studio aesthetic.
- Avoid cartoon characters and primary-color overload.
- Use tactile geometric fraction tiles rather than mascot visuals.

### Risk 3 — The prototype becomes too static

Mitigation:

- Prioritize the Weave lesson activation and fraction tile selection.
- Do not spend time on extra lessons.
- Make Student/Teacher toggle and Export Pack interactive.

### Risk 4 — Education/privacy overclaim

Mitigation:

- No student data.
- No student accounts.
- No automated grades.
- No “fully compliant” language.
- Curriculum alignment is a draft for teacher review.

### Risk 5 — Too much content buries the contest story

Mitigation:

- Hero and Student App Preview must be screenshot-ready.
- Put details in expandable/tabs/cards.
- Keep the main path obvious.

## Recommended cuts if time is tight

Cut first:

- extra sample lesson chips;
- detailed Made with Stitch timeline;
- elaborate printable worksheet content;
- complex scroll animations;
- any external integration simulation.

Do not cut:

- hero;
- weave interaction;
- student app preview;
- teacher console;
- safety/review panel;
- export pack.

## Final red-team verdict

Proceed with Lesson Loom.

The build should win by being emotionally legible, visually rich, responsible, and clearly Stitch-native. The danger is overbuilding. One excellent lesson transformation is stronger than five shallow demos.
